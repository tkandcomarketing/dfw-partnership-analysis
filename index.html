Understanding This Analysis

What is "Risk-Adjusted"?

Rather than assuming perfect conditions every year, risk-adjusted projections account for the statistical probability of disruptions (illness, emergencies, unforeseen events). This provides a more realistic expected outcome than best-case projections.

Why Does Partnership Reduce Risk?

When one provider is unavailable, the other maintains patient flow and revenue continuity. Industry data shows solo practitioners face approximately 7% annual revenue exposure from disruptions, while partnerships reduce this impact by roughly 70%.

Apples-to-Apples Comparison

Both scenarios assume a growth goal of 200 patients per provider and projections are reduced by conservative industry risk-adjusted standards. This ensures a fair, conservative comparison between the two paths.

Methodology & Assumptions

Risk Adjustment Methodology

  • Solo disruption rate: 7% base (industry average for solo practitioners)
  • Aging factor: +1% per year after Year 5
  • Partnership disruption impact: ~30% of solo (70% mitigation through coverage)
  • Conservative scenario: 15% buffer applied to partnership growth projections

Valuation Methodology

  • Solo practice multiple: 0.5-0.6x annual revenue (owner-dependent)
  • Multi-provider practice multiple: 0.7-0.8x annual revenue (transferable)
  • Enterprise value = total practice value regardless of ownership split
  • Owner's equity = enterprise value × ownership percentage (50% at Year 10)

Partnership Structure

Equity transition: 10% per year for 5 years (90/10 → 50/50). Compensation model: $12,000/month guaranteed draw per partner plus profit share based on ownership percentage. Seasonal fluctuations and capacity constraints factored into all projections.
